Devil's Flower Mantis
Extatosoma Tiaratum
Scorpionflies (Mecoptera)
The Devil's Flower Mantis can mimic the colors of the Devil's Flower which is an orchid. This type of Mantis gets stressed easily by being handled or disturbed by pet and humans.
The Extatosoma Tiaratum is a type of stick insect that is covered in thorny spikes. This insect can also release a chemical that smells like peanut butter to humans.
The Scorpionfly looks like a mixture of a scorpion, a fly and a wasp. These types of flies were around since the Mesozoic age. These flies can not sting and the female doesn't even have the "stinger".

Antlion

Known as the doodlebug, the larvae eats ants. The Antlion was called the doodlebug because the larvae creates doodles in the ground.

Scorpion

Scorpions are considered Arachnids and are great survivors. The only thing they can't survive without is soil.

Cranefly

Mistakened for a mosquito, these flies are about 1.5 inches long with long fragile legs.
