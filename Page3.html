Past Insects/Arachnids
M. permiana
J. rhenaniae
Arthropleura
  • Was known to have a wingspan of more than 2ft and a body that was about 17 inches. The M. permiana may have fed on large frogs and squirrels because of its immense size. This creature was known as the biggest insect of all time.
    The J. rhenaniae was known to grow up to 8ft and its pincers were more than 18 inches long. This creature was truly a monstrous sea scorpion. These enormous creatures was said to be big because of the oxygen levels in the atmosphere.
    The Arthropleura was an ancestor to the centipede and millipede and could reach more than 8ft in length. This was the largest invertebrate species that was ever unearthed. This is a creature that you don't want to come across.
